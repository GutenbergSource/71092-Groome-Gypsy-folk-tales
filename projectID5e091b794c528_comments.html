A collection of Gypsy (Romani) folk-tales, preceded with a long introduction giving some details of their history. The tales have been collected all over the world, and the author has added numerous notes to them.

The book is average on the hard side, with numberous footnotes, some fragments in Greek and plenty of accented letters. Section titles have been set as inset notes, with can be rendered as [sidenote: ....], and moved to above the paragraph they appear in.

Have fun!
